# Katakana Practice Exercises

Katakana is the writing system Japanese uses for foreign loanwords, foreign names, onomatopoeia, and technical terms. Once you know it, you can instantly read thousands of borrowed English, French, and Portuguese words in Japanese text. This article gives you concrete exercises — reading drills, writing practice, recognition work on confusable pairs, and a 20-question quiz — so you build genuine skill rather than vague familiarity.

Keep the [katakana chart](/hiragana-katakana-chart) open in a second tab as a reference while you work through the sections.

## Section 1: Reading Practice

The fastest way to gain confidence with katakana is to read real loanwords. You already know the meanings; your only job is to decode the spelling.

Read down the katakana column three times. On the third pass, cover both answer columns and say the English word out loud. If you hesitate on any row, return to it.

## Section 2: Writing Practice

Writing katakana by hand fixes each character's shape in your visual memory. For each character, study the stroke note, write it five times, then write it five more times without looking.

### The Five Vowels

Every katakana word is built from these five sounds plus their combinations.

| Character | Reading | Stroke Notes |
|-----------|---------|--------------|
| ア | a | 2 strokes: a short diagonal line top-left, then a longer diagonal line crossing it from upper-right |
| イ | i | 2 strokes: a short diagonal going down-right, then a longer vertical stroke |
| ウ | u | 3 strokes: a short horizontal line on top, then two strokes forming an open V shape below |
| エ | e | 3 strokes: top horizontal, vertical center, bottom horizontal (like the letter I with serifs) |
| オ | o | 3 strokes: horizontal, then vertical crossing it, then a final diagonal kicking right |

After copying each vowel ten times, write them in sequence — ア、イ、ウ、エ、オ — without looking at the table.

### K-Row and S-Row

| Character | Reading | Stroke Notes |
|-----------|---------|--------------|
| カ | ka | 2 strokes: a slightly angled horizontal, then a long diagonal dropping right |
| キ | ki | 3 strokes: two horizontals, then a vertical crossing both |
| ク | ku | 2 strokes: a short diagonal from top, then a longer curve sweeping right and down |
| ケ | ke | 3 strokes: vertical, then two horizontals crossing it — the lower one angled down-right |
| コ | ko | 2 strokes: top horizontal, then vertical down, then short bottom horizontal (like a squared C) |
| サ | sa | 3 strokes: two short horizontals, then a long diagonal dropping left |
| シ | shi | 3 strokes: two short horizontal dashes on the left, then a long curved stroke sweeping right — the curve opens **to the right** |
| ス | su | 2 strokes: a horizontal on top, then a vertical that sweeps into a curve at the bottom |
| セ | se | 3 strokes: a vertical with a horizontal crossing it and a curved foot |
| ソ | so | 2 strokes: a short diagonal tick, then a long diagonal sweeping down-right — the main stroke is nearly **vertical** |

**Writing challenge:** Write the full k-row (カキクケコ) from memory, then the full s-row (サシスセソ). Check against the chart.

### T-Row and N-Row

| Character | Reading | Stroke Notes |
|-----------|---------|--------------|
| タ | ta | 3 strokes: a horizontal, then a diagonal, then a diagonal crossing the first |
| チ | chi | 3 strokes: two horizontals, then a vertical with a curved foot |
| ツ | tsu | 3 strokes: two short diagonal dashes on the left, then a long curve sweeping right and down — the curve opens **downward and right** |
| テ | te | 3 strokes: a short diagonal, then a horizontal, then a long vertical with a curved foot |
| ト | to | 2 strokes: a vertical, then a short horizontal poking out right from the middle |
| ナ | na | 2 strokes: a horizontal, then a vertical crossing it with a small hook at the bottom |
| ニ | ni | 2 strokes: a short horizontal on top, then a longer horizontal below |
| ヌ | nu | 2 strokes: a diagonal crossing another diagonal, with a small loop where they meet |
| ネ | ne | 4 strokes: complex — work from the chart for correct order |
| ノ | no | 1 stroke: a single diagonal sweeping from upper-left to lower-right |

Copy each character five times. ノ is one of the easiest katakana characters — practice it until it feels natural, as it appears in many common words.

## Section 3: Recognition Exercises

Four pairs of katakana characters cause consistent errors. Each pair shares visual features that make them easy to swap at a glance. This section gives you the tools to tell them apart reliably.

### The Four Problem Pairs

Study each pair side by side. The key difference column is the thing to memorize.

| Pair | Character A | Reading | Character B | Reading | Key Visual Difference |
|------|-------------|---------|-------------|---------|----------------------|
| シ vs ツ | シ | shi | ツ | tsu | **シ** has horizontal dashes and a curve that sweeps **to the right**. **ツ** has diagonal dashes and a curve that sweeps **downward**. Think: シ lies on its side, ツ stands upright. |
| ソ vs ン | ソ | so | ン | n | **ソ** has a short tick and a long stroke going **down-right** (like a /). **ン** has a short tick and a long stroke going **down-left** (like a \\). The angle of the long stroke is the tell. |
| ク vs タ | ク | ku | タ | ta | **ク** is open on the left, with two strokes curving right. **タ** has a horizontal at the top and two crossing diagonals below. ク has no horizontals; タ always starts with one. |
| ワ vs ウ | wa | ワ | u | ウ | **ウ** has a short horizontal stroke **on top**. **ワ** does not — it starts with the curve directly. Count strokes: ウ has 3, ワ has 2. |

### Drill 1: Name the Character

Write the romaji reading for each character below without looking at the table above.

1. シ → ___
2. ソ → ___
3. ツ → ___
4. ン → ___
5. ク → ___
6. ワ → ___
7. タ → ___
8. ウ → ___
9. シ → ___
10. ン → ___

**Answers:** 1. shi, 2. so, 3. tsu, 4. n, 5. ku, 6. wa, 7. ta, 8. u, 9. shi, 10. n

### Drill 2: Pick the Correct Character

Circle the character that matches the reading shown. Do this quickly — you want recognition to become automatic.

- **tsu:** シ / ツ / シ / ツ / ツ → *(Answer: ツ)*
- **n:** ン / ソ / ン / ソ / ン → *(Answer: ン)*
- **ku:** タ / ク / タ / ク / ク → *(Answer: ク)*
- **u:** ワ / ウ / ウ / ワ / ウ → *(Answer: ウ)*

### Drill 3: Spot the Difference in Real Words

Each pair below contains one correct word and one version with a substitution error. Identify which is correct.

| Option A | Option B | Correct? | Word Meaning |
|----------|----------|----------|--------------|
| スシ | スシ | both correct | sushi |
| ツアー | シアー | Option A | tour |
| ソファ | ンファ | Option A | sofa |
| タクシー | クタシー | Option A | taxi |
| ウイスキー | ワイスキー | Option A | whisky |

If you selected Option B for any row, return to the key difference notes above before continuing.

## Section 7: Daily Practice Routine

Katakana rewards short, consistent sessions. The approach below distributes the 46 basic characters across two weeks, with deliberate attention to the confusable pairs.

### Week 1: Basic Characters

**Days 1–2: Vowels and K-row**
- Write ア、イ、ウ、エ、オ ten times each
- Write カ、キ、ク、ケ、コ five times each
- Read five words from Section 1 aloud

**Days 3–4: S-row and T-row**
- Write サ、シ、ス、セ、ソ five times each
- Write タ、チ、ツ、テ、ト five times each
- Do Drill 1 and Drill 2 from Section 3 (シ/ツ and ソ/ン pairs)

**Days 5–7: N-row through W-row**
- Write ナ、ニ、ヌ、ネ、ノ、ハ、ヒ、フ、ヘ、ホ five times each
- Write マ、ミ、ム、メ、モ、ヤ、ユ、ヨ five times each
- Write ラ、リ、ル、レ、ロ、ワ、ヲ、ン five times each
- Do the ク/タ and ワ/ウ drills from Section 3

### Week 2: Consolidation and Speed

**Days 8–10: Word building**
- Complete Exercise A and Exercise B from Section 4 each day
- Read all loanwords in Section 1 from the katakana column only, no romaji
- Play Round 1 of the guessing game (Section 5) and check your score

**Days 11–12: Harder loanwords**
- Work through Rounds 2 and 3 of Section 5
- Write five katakana words you encounter in daily life (product labels, apps, menus)

**Days 13–14: Full review**
- Write the entire katakana chart from memory: ア行 through ワ行 plus ン
- Take the mini quiz in Section 6 and record your score
- Return to any rows where you scored below 80%

### Daily Habit: The Three-Word Method

Each day, pick three katakana words you encounter in your environment — a product package, a website, a menu, a movie title — and read them aloud. This keeps the script connected to real usage rather than making it feel like abstract study.

### Tracking Your Progress

Keep a list in a notebook or phone with two columns:

- **Confident:** Characters you read and write without hesitation
- **Still learning:** Characters that require thought or cause errors

Move characters between columns as your accuracy improves. When シ, ツ, ソ, and ン are all in the confident column without exception, your recognition of the hardest pairs is solid.

## Next Steps

Once you can read the 46 basic katakana characters reliably, the natural extensions are:

- **Dakuon:** Voiced variants using the ゛mark — ガ (ga), ジ (ji), ズ (zu), バ (ba), etc.
- **Handakuon:** The ゜mark for p-sounds — パ (pa), ピ (pi), プ (pu), ペ (pe), ポ (po)
- **Youon:** Compound sounds — キャ (kya), チュ (chu), ショ (sho), ファ (fa), ウィ (wi)
- **Special extensions:** Katakana uses combinations like ティ (ti), ファ (fa), ウェ (we) for sounds that do not exist in native Japanese — these appear in loanwords from English and other languages

Use the [katakana chart](/hiragana-katakana-chart) to study the full character set including dakuon and youon. When you are ready to test yourself under timed conditions, the [interactive quiz](/hiragana-katakana-quiz) covers all character types and lets you focus on katakana specifically.
